🕸️ How to Recover a Website from Archive.org

So your website’s gone AWOL. Great. All those witty blog posts, pictures of bread, and 3-year-old memes – vanished. Fear not, digital Indiana Jones – Archive.org’s Wayback Machine has your back (most of the time).

Step-by-step recovery guide

  1. Go to archive.org/web.
  2. Paste the lost website URL. Breathe. Hit Enter.
  3. Select a snapshot. (Pro tip: Choose one before your site turned into a 404 party.)
  4. Use FileFossil.io to extract the good bits. Pages, images, documents, dignity...
  5. Export to ZIP. Celebrate like it’s 1999 – because frankly, it might be.

Why not just click “Save As”?

Because you’re not a caveman. Static saves miss assets, links break, styles vanish. FileFossil digs up all the bones and pieces them back together, Jurassic Park-style.
