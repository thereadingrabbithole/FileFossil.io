🧙 The Wayback Machine Isn’t Black Magic

You typed in a dead website and suddenly you’re back in 2006. Frogs had blogs. Flash was still a thing. You didn’t know how it worked – you just whispered, “sorcery” and clicked.

But here’s the deal:

The Wayback Machine stores snapshots of websites over time, based on crawls and user submissions. Some are complete. Some are as broken as your New Year’s resolution.

What it’s good for:

  • Recovering lost websites
  • Tracking changes to content or design
  • Proving you did post that blog before they copied it

Where FileFossil comes in:

We extract every file, page, and image from a snapshot. No manual clicking, no swearing at 404s, no nonsense.
